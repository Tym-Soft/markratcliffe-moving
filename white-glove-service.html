White-Glove Removals for Sussex’s Most Particular Customers

Get a Free Quote Call 01323 848 008

Our white-glove removals service is designed for homes where furniture is bought to live with for decades, artwork is original, and the interior is the result of a deliberate design process. The fundamentals — survey, pad-wrap, lorry, crew — are the same. What changes is the level of inventory detail, the speciality of the crew, the materials we use, the documentation we generate before and after, and the insurance we elect.

What “white-glove” actually means in practice

The term gets thrown around. Here’s the specific list of what changes when you elect our white-glove service over our standard removal:

  • Pre-move condition survey. A senior surveyor walks every room with you, photographs every item of furniture, every artwork and every fragile, and produces a written condition report. You sign it, we sign it — that’s the baseline for any insurance claim if anything does go wrong.
  • Cotton-glove handling for art and silver. Hence the name. Skin oils and sweat damage gilt frames, varnished antiques and polished silver. Our crew wears clean cotton or nitrile gloves whenever handling fine items.
  • Specialist crew. White-glove jobs go to our senior, long-tenured crew — the same people who handle our overseas BAR Overseas Group jobs and our antiques moves. No casual labour, no agency staff.
  • Premium materials. Acid-free archival tissue for art and silver. Anti-static bubble for electronics. Climate-stable foam for fragile ceramics. Custom timber crating where any individual item warrants it.
  • Detailed inventory. Every box, every wrapped item is numbered and recorded on a digital inventory with a thumbnail photo. You get the inventory by email the same day so you can check anything off as it arrives.
  • Premium insurance. All-risks transit cover at declared value, with no per-item cap. We arrange this for you on top of our standard goods-in-transit policy.
  • Post-move walk-around. Once the lorry is empty, our surveyor walks every room with you again, ticks off the inventory, photographs anything that’s arrived less than perfectly, and triggers any claim there and then. No surprises three weeks later.

Who chooses white-glove

It’s a small but consistent slice of our business. Customer profiles include:

  • Country-house and estate moves where the contents include genuine antiques, art and a serious wine collection.
  • London-to-Sussex relocations of designer-built interiors (Lutyens, Brodie, Kelly Hoppen, Christopher Hall — we’ve done them all).
  • Bank, hedge fund and senior-executive household relocations where confidentiality and presentation matter as much as the move itself.
  • Authors, artists, musicians and academics relocating libraries, archives and instruments with insurance values into the six figures.
  • Estate executors handling probate properties where the contents need full documentation before being moved into storage or onward.

Differences vs our standard premium service

White-glove

Pre-move condition report · Item-level inventory with thumbnails · Cotton-glove handling · Acid-free materials · All-risks insurance · Post-move walk-around · Same senior crew door-to-door.

The price difference is typically 25–40% over a standard premium quote. For homes where the contents value runs into six figures it’s genuinely cheaper than a single insurance claim.

How the white-glove process works

  1. Initial survey. Conducted by Mark personally for larger jobs. Walks every room, asks about provenance, photographs everything that warrants individual attention.
  2. Written quote and contract. Fixed price, all-inclusive. No day-of surprises.
  3. Pre-move pack week. Larger moves get a 3–5 day pack window. Materials staged onsite. Each room packed top-to-bottom by the same crew so accountability is clear.
  4. Move day(s). Some white-glove jobs run over 2–3 days because we deliberately don’t rush. The lorry is loaded by category — art first, fragile next, furniture last.
  5. Delivery. Floor protection laid down in the destination property before the first box arrives. Items unwrapped in the room where they’ll live. Boxes opened only by request.
  6. Walk-around and sign-off. Surveyor returns at end of day, walks every room with you, confirms everything against the inventory.

High-value insurance arranged for you

Our standard goods-in-transit cover is excellent for ordinary household contents but caps per item at £40/kg. For white-glove jobs we always pair it with all-risks transit cover at declared value. The premium is typically 0.5–1.2% of the declared contents value — on a £200,000 contents that’s £1,000–£2,400, which sounds material until you compare it to the cost of replacing a single damaged Persian rug or torn canvas. We arrange the policy for you and email the certificate before move day. If you already have art-specific insurance (Hiscox, Aon, NFU Mutual), we’ll work with your existing broker on the wording.

Our signature method

The full pad-wrap process — in three steps

This is what makes the biggest difference to whether your possessions arrive intact.

  1. 01

    Wrap in your home

    Every piece of furniture is pad-wrapped where it stands. Drawer fronts taped, corners protected, fragile detail covered — before anything leaves the room.

  2. 02

    Loaded stack-safe

    Wrapped pieces go straight to the lorry and are loaded in a stack-safe sequence, strapped to the bulkhead. They never get unwrapped in transit.

  3. 03

    Unwrap in final position

    At your new home, pieces are carried in still wrapped, placed in the room and the exact position you choose, and only then unwrapped. The result: damage-free arrival.

Frequently asked questions

How much does white-glove removals cost?

It is genuinely bespoke. As a rough yardstick, expect 25–40% above our standard premium quote for the equivalent house. For a 4-bed country home with high-value contents that typically runs £3,500–£8,500 in Sussex; significantly more for full estate moves. Every white-glove job is quoted after a personal survey by Mark.

Do you require an inventory list from me?

You don’t have to provide one — we build the inventory ourselves during the pre-move survey. If you already have one (for insurance purposes), we’ll cross-reference and confirm.

Can you ship art to/from overseas under white-glove?

Yes. We’re a BAR Overseas Group member and routinely handle international art shipments alongside household moves. Custom timber crates, ISPM-15 compliant, with the documentation needed for customs at both ends.

How do you handle confidentiality?

Every crew member signs a written confidentiality clause as part of their employment. For high-profile moves we run the job under a project code rather than the customer name on internal paperwork. NDAs available on request — we’ll sign yours or use ours.

Are you DBS-checked?

Our senior crew (the people who staff white-glove jobs) are all enhanced-DBS-checked. We can provide certificates ahead of the move on request.
