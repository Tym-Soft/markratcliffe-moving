Helpful Tips for Your Sussex Move

Get a Free Quote Call 01323 848 008

Moving home is one of the three most stressful things people do (after divorce and bereavement, depending on the study). It doesn’t have to be. Most of the stress comes from the fifty small decisions that pile up in the last fortnight — not from the move itself. The tips below are the ones we wish every customer had read three weeks before move day. Read what’s relevant, ignore the rest, and don’t panic.

Six weeks out

The work you do six weeks out is what makes move week feel calm. Concrete things to handle now:

  • Book your remover. Better movers fill up 6–10 weeks ahead, especially for end-of-month dates. We always recommend getting a survey done first, because the survey will surface things (a piano, a pond, a cat that lives in the loft) that change the quote.
  • Start decluttering room by room. One room a week, in 30-minute blocks. Anything you haven’t used in 18 months is a candidate for donation or sale.
  • Get a floor-plan of the new property. Mark where each piece of furniture will go. You will save hours on move day if the crew knows which corner the wardrobe lives in.
  • Tell the schools. If you’re moving outside the catchment, give the new schools time. Most Sussex primary schools want at least a half-term’s notice; many secondary schools more.
  • Start your address-change list. Council, doctor, dentist, optician, vet, gym, bank, building society, employer, HMRC, DVLA, pension, insurance (car/home/life), TV licence, energy, water, broadband, mobile, streaming, every shop loyalty card. Use the moving checklist — we’ve done the list for you.

Four weeks out

  • Confirm the move date in writing. Especially if it’s contingent on a chain. We’re used to chains and don’t bill you if a date slides — talk to us as soon as you know.
  • Order packing materials. If self-packing, our materials page lists what to order and how much you’ll need. Better to have a few too many than to run out at midnight.
  • Empty the freezer. Stop refilling it now. The contents of a chest freezer are usually thrown out on move day — they’ve thawed by the time the new home’s freezer is on.
  • Disconnect anything specialist. Wall-mounted TVs, satellite dishes, security alarms, smart-home hubs. Most need a Gas Safe engineer or an electrician on the day — book them now.
  • Inventory the loft. Lofts are usually where surprises live (the Christmas tree, the suitcase of receipts, the box of family photos). Get up there now, decide what’s coming and what’s going.

Two weeks out

  • Pack rooms you don’t use daily. Spare bedroom, dining room (if you don’t eat there), back garden, loft, garage. Don’t touch the kitchen or main bedroom yet.
  • Confirm parking and access. Tell us if a parking suspension is needed. We’ll arrange it with the council if it’s a permit zone — this takes 5–10 working days to authorise.
  • Take meter readings. Gas, electric and water at both addresses on the day of completion. Photograph them with the date stamp on.
  • Inform pet boarders. If your dog/cat is staying with someone on move day, confirm dates now. We strongly recommend pets not be on site during the move — lorries, strangers and open doors are a stressful combination.
  • Cash in any vouchers. Local-business gift cards, restaurant credit, leisure-centre memberships — either spend them or transfer them.

The week of the move

  • Pack a “first night” box. Kettle, mugs, tea, coffee, sugar, milk, bedding for one bed, towels, toiletries, phone chargers, painkillers, snacks, basic cutlery and plates for one meal. Carry it in your own car. It is the single most useful thing you can do.
  • Defrost the fridge and freezer. 24 hours before move day. Put a towel in the bottom to soak up the meltwater.
  • Photograph the existing setup of your TV, internet router and any cabled electronics. Unboxing the new home is much easier when you know which cable goes where.
  • Charge everything. Phones, laptops, power banks, vape devices. You will not find your charger for at least a day.
  • Bag the essentials separately. Passports, birth certificates, the dog’s vaccination record, keys (old and new), the conveyancer’s contact details. These travel with you, not in the lorry.

Move day

  • Be there when we arrive. 8.00–8.30 typically. Coffee for the crew is appreciated but not required.
  • Walk through with the crew leader. Confirm what’s moving and what’s staying. Point out anything especially fragile or unusual.
  • Final meter readings. Before the new occupants’ clock starts.
  • Leave a note for the new owners. Where the meter cupboard is, when the bin day is, what the broadband password might be. Small kindnesses go a long way.
  • Don’t supervise the lorry. Let the crew load. They’ve done this thousands of times. Your job is to handle the new property arrival and direct the unload.

Settling in — week one in your new Sussex home

  • Find the stopcock and the consumer unit. Before you need them. Saves a panicked search at 2am.
  • Test smoke and CO alarms. Press and hold the button on each. Replace any battery that beeps.
  • Bleed the radiators. Particularly relevant for older Sussex properties. Air pockets accumulate when central heating sits idle.
  • Register with the GP and dentist. Done in week one rather than week four — appointments can be 4–8 weeks out.
  • Sign up for council services. Bin collection, recycling rota, parking permits, garden waste. Each Sussex council has different rules.
  • Walk the neighbourhood. Find the nearest shop, post box, bus stop and your closest pub. The fastest way to feel at home.

Money-saving moving tips

  • Move mid-week, mid-month. Tuesday-Wednesday of the second or third week of any month is the cheapest. Friday end-of-month is the most expensive.
  • Avoid August. School-summer holiday weeks have surcharges across the industry. June or September often saves 10–15%.
  • Self-pack the easy stuff. Books, clothes, linen — pack these yourself. Let the professionals do the kitchen and the fragile.
  • Use second-hand boxes. Our materials page offers used cartons at ~40% off new.
  • Sell, donate or recycle before you move — not after. You pay to move everything that comes with you. The cubic-foot-pence add up.
