How to use the calculator

Walk through your home room by room. For each item we include — there are 89 common household items in the list — type the quantity you own. The totals box at the top updates instantly. Three things to remember:

  • Estimates, not exact. Our crews see the same item dimensions vary by 10-20%. The figures here are the BAR-style averages we use to plan a Sussex job; they get you in the right ballpark.
  • Allow for boxes. A typical three-bedroom Sussex house has 60-80 standard removal boxes once everything is packed; if you don’t know yet, allow 75 tea-chest-sized boxes for planning purposes.
  • Storage versus lorry. The same volume of contents takes a different shape in a storage container than on a lorry — your storage quote and your removals quote may both reference the cu ft figure but the practical implications differ. We’ll explain on the survey.

Lorry-size guide for Mark Ratcliffe Moving jobs

Our crews use these rough cubic-footage bands to choose which lorry leaves the depot on the day. The calculator above gives a load-size estimate based on the same bands:

  • Up to 80 cu ft — a small van load. A single-person move, a few items only.
  • 80-250 cu ft — Luton van load. A small flat or one-bedroom property.
  • 250-600 cu ft — 3.5-tonne lorry. A two-bedroom flat or small house.
  • 600-1100 cu ft — 7.5-tonne lorry. A typical three- to four-bedroom Sussex home.
  • 1100-1700 cu ft — 18-tonne lorry. A large four-bedroom house with contents.
  • 1700+ cu ft — articulated lorry or multiple loads. A substantial country property with antiques and outbuildings.
