Why the blog exists

Two reasons:

First, because most removals industry content online is generic, AI-written and clearly hasn’t been near a real moving day in years. We’ve seen the same blog post about “tips for moving day” cut-and-pasted onto a thousand removals websites. We’re not going to do that to you. Every post on this blog comes from our actual experience, written by Mark or one of the team who’s been on the lorry for at least a decade.

Second, because the conversations we have with customers at survey time keep covering the same ground. If we write it down properly — once — then customers can find it when they need it, and we can spend the survey time talking about the specifics of your move, not the generalities of the industry.

Subscribe or follow

We don’t do an email newsletter (we don’t want to spam you) and we don’t do paid social. If you want to know when new articles go up, the best options are:

  • Follow our Facebook page — we post new articles there and occasionally share customer-permission photos from interesting moves: Mark Ratcliffe Moving on Facebook.
  • Bookmark the blog index page — new posts go up there first.
  • Subscribe to the RSS feed — for the technically inclined. The feed URL is /blog/feed.xml.

Guest contributions

We occasionally publish posts by industry friends — antiques dealers, Sussex auction houses, a piano tuner we work with, an estate-agent we trust. If you have something honest and useful to say to people planning a Sussex move, email office@markratcliffemoving.co.uk with the angle you have in mind. We don’t pay for guest posts and we don’t accept anything that’s really an ad. But genuinely useful stuff written by someone who knows their subject — yes.
