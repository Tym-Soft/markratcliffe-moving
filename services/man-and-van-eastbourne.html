Not every move needs a 7.5-tonne lorry and a full pre-pack.   • IKEA or furniture-shop collections — let us pick up your flatpacks while you carry on with your day

How man-and-van differs from a full removal — and which is right for you

Customers sometimes ask whether man-and-van is a "cheaper version" of a full removal. The truthful answer is no — it is a different service for a different job. A full removal is a fixed-price, planned, surveyed move with packing, dedicated lorry capacity and a structured day. Man-and-van is an hourly-billed, smaller-scale service for jobs where a surveyed quote would be overkill. For a 1-bedroom flat move within Eastbourne, man-and-van is almost always cheaper and entirely appropriate. For a 3-bedroom house move, man-and-van billed hourly typically ends up more expensive (and more stressful) than a properly quoted full removal — because the hours add up faster than people expect.

Man-and-van jobs we run every week

These are the kinds of jobs that make up the majority of our man-and-van bookings around Eastbourne — and the kinds of jobs full-service movers either decline or quote unreasonably high for.

  • Studio and bedsit moves — typically 2-3 hours with a Luton van and one helper plus driver
  • Single-item collections — a sofa from Sussex Furniture, a wardrobe from Marketplace, a fridge from John Lewis
  • Pre-purchase pickups — collecting a private-sale item before completion at your new home
  • Garage and shed clearances — taking workshop kit, bikes and tools to a new property or to charity
  • End-of-tenancy moves — small flat moves where the tenant manages most of the packing
  • Auction collections — picking up purchases from local auction houses
  • Storage transfers — moving items between your home and our Prestige rooms
  • Inter-property shifts — moving rooms of furniture between two properties you own
  • Light office moves — single-room offices, home office equipment

How to keep your man-and-van booking efficient

Because the service is billed hourly, the things you can do before the crew arrives translate directly into pounds saved:

  • Be packed before the van arrives. Everything in boxes, taped shut, stacked near the front door. The crew should not be waiting for you to pack.
  • Pre-dismantle awkward furniture. Beds, flat-pack wardrobes and large IKEA pieces are faster if you have already dismantled them. Keep the fixings in labelled bags.
  • Park-clear at both ends. If parking right outside is uncertain, move your own car earlier to keep the space.
  • Have a destination plan. Know which room each item is going to. Tell the crew on arrival — saves "where do you want this?" trips.

Property types we move in Eastbourne

Eastbourne properties tend to fall into two main groups. Substantial detached commuter homes on the established estates — typically three to five bedrooms, with garages, summerhouses, gym or office conversions and a fair amount of outdoor furniture. And the older village cottages and farmhouses in the surrounding lanes — listed buildings, original fireplaces, low beams and steep stairs that benefit from careful pad-wrap protection on every piece.

Either type lends itself well to a proper in-home survey. We walk every room, count cartons by name and size, photograph the access points and email the quote with itemised pricing within two working days. For straightforward smaller jobs (a single bedroom, a studio flat or a baggage shipment) a phone or video survey is enough. The aim is the same regardless of the property: a fixed, written price that doesn’t move on the day.

Routes and journey times from Eastbourne

Our daily routes out of the Lower Dicker depot put Eastbourne within standard half-day or full-day reach, depending on inventory size and the destination address. A typical Eastbourne pickup with a Sussex or Surrey delivery completes in a single day, including pad-wrapping at the load and unwrapping at the unload. The lorry leaves Lower Dicker in time to be on your driveway ten minutes before the stated load time — we don’t make customers wait around at 6am for a 7am scheduled start.

For chain-day moves out of Eastbourne the timings matter and we plan the schedule from the keys-release time backwards. The conveyancer’s 12 noon or 1pm completion target shapes when we crew up, when we pad-wrap and when we leave the property. For longer-distance UK moves — to the Midlands, the West Country, the north — we generally split the day across two crews if the inventory exceeds a single lorry. Anything heading overseas from Eastbourne routes through our customs-controlled holding bay at the depot, where the container is loaded after a final inventory check.

Our quote and survey process for Eastbourne

Every Eastbourne quote starts with an in-home or video survey — free, no obligation, and the only way to give you a fixed price that doesn’t move on move day. The surveyor walks every room, photographs access at both ends, and counts the cartons by size. The written quote follows within 48 hours, broken down by line: crew time, lorry, fuel, pad-wrap, materials, insurance, any specialist handling. You can see exactly what every cost line is paying for.

Confirming the date takes a 20-25% deposit, protected under the BAR Advance Payment Guarantee. The balance is paid on the day of completion. The crew that arrives on move day is the crew that gets the job done from start to finish — uniformed, directly employed, trained at our Sussex training centre. No agency labour, no day rates, no sub-contractors. It’s a slightly more expensive way to run a removals firm but it’s the single biggest reason we’ve been in business since 1982.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much is your man and van service in Eastbourne?

Our hourly rate starts from £55 per hour for one mover plus driver, with a 2-hour minimum. A weekend or evening surcharge applies. Larger vans and additional movers are quoted separately.

Can you do same-day man and van in Eastbourne?

Sometimes — it depends on our diary. We do not promise same-day in advance; call us and we will tell you honestly what we can fit in today.

What is the difference between man-and-van and a full removal?

Man-and-van is hourly, uses a smaller vehicle (Luton or 3.5-tonne), suits small flats, single items and student moves. A full removal is quoted as a fixed job, uses larger lorries and includes our full pad-wrap protection and packing service.

Do you supply boxes for man-and-van jobs?

Yes — we can drop materials in advance or you can collect from our Lower Dicker depot. Pad-wrap and blankets are included with the vehicle.

Can I use man-and-van for an IKEA collection?

Absolutely — IKEA flat-pack collections, eBay pickups and Facebook Marketplace runs are some of our most common man-and-van jobs. Tell us the address and dimensions and we will quote.
