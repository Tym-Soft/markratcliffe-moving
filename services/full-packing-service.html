Full Packing Service — We Pack Every Last Thing

Get a Free Quote Call 01323 848 008

Our full packing service is the option to choose when you simply do not want to think about packing — you’d rather sleep in your own bed the night before the move with everything still on the shelves where it lives, and come home the day after to find a fully unpacked house. We arrive one or two days ahead of the removals lorry, pack absolutely everything in your home into removal-grade cartons, pad-wrap every piece of furniture in situ, and label every box with its destination room. All materials are included in the quoted price.

What “full packing” actually includes

It is genuinely everything. Not just the obvious breakables, not just the kitchen china. Every drawer, every cupboard, every shelf, every wardrobe. To give you a sense of scale, here’s a representative inventory for a typical full pack of a 3-bedroom semi-detached home in Eastbourne:

  • Kitchen. Every cup, plate, glass and bowl individually tissue-wrapped. Pots and pans nested with paper between. Small appliances individually bubble-wrapped. The contents of every drawer including utensils and Tupperware. Pantry items in sealed cartons with leak-resistant liners. Oven trays and grills bagged. Wine racks wrapped and crated.
  • Living room. Books in triple-wall book boxes (small, because books are heavy). DVDs, vinyl, magazines and games into bespoke media cartons. Ornaments and photographs individually tissue-wrapped. Lamp shades separated from bases. TVs in original boxes where available, otherwise corner-padded cartons. Console games and remote controls in labelled bags.
  • Dining room. Crystal and stemware standing upright in dividered cartons with tissue stuffing inside each glass. Silver wrapped in anti-tarnish tissue. Place settings and serving ware nested with paper between.
  • Bedrooms. Hanging clothes directly from your wardrobe into our wardrobe cartons (built-in hanging rail — no folding). Folded clothes in linen cartons. Bedding into sealed mattress bags. Books in book boxes. Bedside-table contents in labelled cartons.
  • Bathrooms. Toiletries in sealed plastic bags inside cartons (in case of leaks). Medicines kept separate for you to carry personally. Towels and linens bagged.
  • Loft, garage, shed. Christmas decorations, suitcases, tools, garden equipment. Lawnmowers drained of fuel and oil. Bikes wrapped and strapped. Paint and chemicals handled separately under hazardous-goods rules.
  • Office and study. Files into archive cartons. Computers and monitors in anti-static bubble. Cables coiled, labelled and bagged. Photographs taken of cable arrangements before disconnection so you can rebuild your setup easily.
  • Furniture. Every piece pad-wrapped in heavy quilted blankets in situ — before it leaves the room. Drawer fronts taped. Glass tops and detachable parts wrapped separately.

How a full pack day actually runs

  1. Pre-move survey. We visit beforehand to walk through every room, agree what is moving and what is staying, photograph anything already damaged (so we can hand it over in identical condition), and decide whether you need one or two pack days.
  2. Pack day 1. Crew arrives at 8.30am. Floor protection laid down. We start with the rooms you use least: lofts, sheds, spare bedrooms, china cabinets. By the end of pack day 1 the back rooms, kitchen china and most ornaments are boxed.
  3. Pack day 2 (for larger homes). Living-room electronics, main wardrobes, bathroom essentials and kitchen items you want to keep using until move day. We leave you what we call a “live kit” — one carton with kettle, mugs, plates for the morning, plus essential bathroom items.
  4. Move day. Lorry arrives. Everything that’s packed is loaded. We pack the live kit last, then load it last so you can find it first at the other end.
  5. Delivery and placement. Boxes are placed into the correct rooms based on the colour-coded labels we put on each one when packing.
  6. Optional unpack. Add the unpacking service and we’ll unpack the boxes into agreed locations and take the cardboard away the same day.

Materials included — nothing extra to buy

The price we quote for a full pack includes every material we use. You don’t buy boxes on the side, you don’t pay extra for the wardrobe cartons or the picture cartons. For reference, a typical 3-bedroom full pack consumes:

  • 40–60 standard book and linen cartons
  • 10–15 large cartons for clothes and soft furnishings
  • 6–10 wardrobe cartons
  • 2–4 picture/mirror telescopic cartons
  • 1 large roll bubble wrap, 4–6 packs tissue paper
  • 8–12 rolls vinyl tape
  • 20–30 quilted furniture blankets (these are ours — we take them away)
  • Mattress bags, sofa bags, electronics anti-static bubble as needed

If you were to buy that lot retail it would cost you £300–£450. With our full pack it is included.

Who chooses the full packing service

Most of our full-pack customers fit one of these profiles:

  • Both partners working full-time. Nobody has two weekends to spare. The cost of professional packing is less than the hours of lost work.
  • Downsizers in their 60s and 70s. Pad-wrapping a chest of drawers is genuinely hard work physically. We do it without you needing to lift a thing.
  • International relocations. Export packing is technical (ISPM-15 timber compliance, anti-mould treatment, customs documentation). It is not a DIY job.
  • Insurance-sensitive customers. Most goods-in-transit policies require professional packing for the high-value items. Self-packed boxes are often excluded.
  • People with very fragile collections. Art, ceramics, glassware, antiques. Get it wrong once and you’ve lost something irreplaceable.

How much does a full pack cost?

Honest guide prices for full packing in Sussex, including all materials:

  • 1-bed flat: £220–£380. Half-day with two packers.
  • 2-bed flat or maisonette: £320–£520. Full day with two packers.
  • 3-bed semi or terraced: £480–£720. 1–2 days with two packers.
  • 4-bed detached: £680–£980. 2 days with two or three packers.
  • 5+ bed country home: from £980. Custom quoted after a survey.

The price varies depending on the contents (a house full of antiques takes longer than a minimalist new-build), the access (steep stairs slow the crew) and the time of year (the August and end-of-month peak weeks have a slight surcharge).

Our signature method

The full pad-wrap process — in three steps

This is what makes the biggest difference to whether your possessions arrive intact.

  1. 01

    Wrap in your home

    Every piece of furniture is pad-wrapped where it stands. Drawer fronts taped, corners protected, fragile detail covered — before anything leaves the room.

  2. 02

    Loaded stack-safe

    Wrapped pieces go straight to the lorry and are loaded in a stack-safe sequence, strapped to the bulkhead. They never get unwrapped in transit.

  3. 03

    Unwrap in final position

    At your new home, pieces are carried in still wrapped, placed in the room and the exact position you choose, and only then unwrapped. The result: damage-free arrival.

Frequently asked questions

How early before move day do you pack?

Typically 1–2 days ahead. Smaller homes get a single pack day the day before move day. Larger homes or homes with significant fragile contents get two pack days finishing the day before the move.

Can I keep using the kitchen and bedroom while you pack?

Yes. We leave you a “live kit” on pack day — one carton with the kettle, mugs, plates, basic cutlery, plus bathroom essentials and a kettle. Everything else gets packed; the live kit gets packed last and unpacked first.

Are materials really included? No extras?

Yes. Every box, every roll of bubble wrap, every roll of tape and every blanket is included in the quoted price. If you don’t use everything we leave you, we take the rest back. You will not get a bill for materials on top of the quote.

Do you pack the contents of drawers?

Yes. For lightweight drawers we may leave the contents in situ and tape the drawer fronts shut to save you unpacking. For heavy or fragile drawers, we empty into a labelled carton so the furniture can be moved safely.

What about my high-value items?

Art, antiques, jewellery and very high-value items are inventoried individually with a written condition report and (where relevant) photographs. Anything above your standard insurance excess gets logged separately so the additional cover applies.

Will you pack hazardous goods?

No — legally we cannot transport paint, petrol, gas bottles, fireworks, ammunition, propane heaters, BBQ fuel or compressed air canisters. We’ll talk through these at the survey so you can dispose of them properly or carry them yourself.

Can I help pack to save money?

Of course — that’s our fragile-only packing option or just buy materials from us. Many customers self-pack the easy stuff (clothes, books, linen) and have us do the kitchen, fragile and the wardrobe pack.
