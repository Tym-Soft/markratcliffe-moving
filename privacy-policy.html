Privacy Policy

Get a Free Quote Call 01323 848 008

This privacy policy explains how Mark Ratcliffe Moving & Storage (operating as EMV London Ltd, registered in England) collects, uses and protects personal data when you contact us, request a quote, book a removal or use this website. We take your privacy seriously and only process data we genuinely need to provide the services you ask us for, in line with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

Who we are

Data controller: EMV London Ltd, trading as Mark Ratcliffe Moving & Storage.

Registered office: Unit J12 Swallow Business Park, Diamond Drive, Lower Dicker, East Sussex BN27 4EL.

Contact for privacy matters: mark@markratcliffemoving.co.uk or 01323 848 008.

We are registered with the Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO).

What personal data we collect

We only collect the data we need to quote, plan and carry out your removal. Specifically:

  • Contact details — name, postal addresses (collection and delivery), email, phone numbers.
  • Quote and move details — property type, room count, inventory of items being moved, access details (parking, stairs, lift), preferred date, special requirements.
  • Survey photographs — where you ask us to survey your property, we may photograph rooms and items for quoting purposes, with your permission.
  • Payment information — we never store full card numbers on our systems. Card payments are processed by our card-payment provider (currently Stripe), who hold PCI-DSS certification.
  • Communication history — emails, call records and notes from conversations relating to your enquiry or booking.
  • Website analytics — aggregated, anonymised data from Google Analytics about how visitors use this site. No personally identifiable information.

Why we collect it (lawful basis)

We rely on three lawful bases under UK GDPR:

  • Contract — the data is necessary to deliver the removal you’ve asked us to quote for or carry out. Without it we cannot quote, plan or execute the move.
  • Legitimate interest — for business administration, customer-service follow-up after a move, retention of records for insurance and tax compliance, fraud prevention.
  • Consent — for marketing emails (which we very rarely send) and for using customer photos in our gallery or testimonials. You can withdraw consent at any time.

Who we share data with

We share the minimum data necessary, only with:

  • Our delivery partners — for international moves, our BAR Overseas Group partner at the destination needs your delivery address, contents inventory and customs declaration. For Sussex deliveries to our own depot, no third party is involved.
  • Insurance providers — where you have elected additional all-risks transit cover or storage insurance, we share the schedule and declared value with the underwriter.
  • Payment processors — Stripe and our bank, for card and bank-transfer payments.
  • HMRC and Companies House — for tax and statutory accounting purposes only.
  • Our website hosting and analytics providers — GitHub Pages, Google Analytics, Google Tag Manager. None of these receive your personally identifiable enquiry information; only anonymised website behaviour data.

We never sell personal data, hand it to marketing companies, or pass it to anyone outside this list without your specific consent.

How long we keep data

  • Quote-only enquiries (no booking): deleted after 24 months.
  • Booked moves: retained for 7 years from move date (HMRC and insurance requirement), then deleted.
  • International moves: retained for 7 years (customs and shipping records).
  • Marketing-consent emails: until you unsubscribe, then deleted.
  • Server logs and analytics: aggregated, anonymised, retained for 26 months.

Your rights under UK GDPR

You have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold on you (Subject Access Request — free of charge, fulfilled within 30 days).
  • Rectify any inaccurate data.
  • Erase your data (“right to be forgotten”), subject to our legal retention obligations.
  • Restrict processing of your data while we resolve any dispute.
  • Object to processing based on legitimate interest.
  • Portability — receive your data in a machine-readable format.
  • Withdraw consent at any time for any consent-based processing.
  • Complain to the Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO) if you believe we’ve mishandled your data — ico.org.uk.

To exercise any of these rights, email mark@markratcliffemoving.co.uk — please include enough information for us to identify you, and we’ll respond within 30 days.

Cookies on this website

We use a small number of cookies, all of which are either strictly necessary or set by our analytics provider:

  • _ga, _gid (Google Analytics): measure how visitors use the site. Anonymised. Expire after 2 years (or session).
  • Strictly necessary cookies: session tokens for our quote form. Expire on browser close.

We do not use advertising cookies, tracking pixels for retargeting, or third-party social cookies (other than the embedded Yoshki BAR badge, which sets one cookie for its own analytics).

You can disable all cookies via your browser settings. The site will work without them, though analytics will not register your visit.

Data security

  • Encryption in transit. This website is served over HTTPS. All email between you and us is over TLS-encrypted SMTP.
  • Encryption at rest. Customer files and contact records are stored on encrypted business systems.
  • Access controls. Only the directly-employed Mark Ratcliffe Moving team have access to customer data. Crew on the day see only the move-relevant inventory.
  • DBS-checked senior staff. Our senior crew (the staff for white-glove and probate jobs) are enhanced-DBS-checked.
  • Incident response. If we detect a data breach affecting your information, we will notify you and the ICO within 72 hours as required by UK GDPR.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. The “effective from” date at the top reflects the last update. Material changes will be flagged on this page for at least 60 days. If we change the way we use your data in a way you didn’t agree to, we’ll contact you for fresh consent.
