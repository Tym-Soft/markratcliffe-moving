Removals in Brighton — Trusted Movers Since 1982

Get a Free Quote Call 01323 848 008

Removals in Brighton are a particular kind of work. The streets are steep, parking is permit-controlled almost everywhere, the terraces have narrow doors and tight stairwells, and the city has a fortnight-by-fortnight rhythm of student moves layered on top of family relocations. Mark Ratcliffe Moving has been running Brighton removals since 1982, with crews who know the difference between a Kemp Town basement flat and a Seven Dials maisonette, and who know which side of Queen’s Park you need a smaller van for.

Areas of Brighton we cover

Our Brighton removals service covers all BN1, BN2, BN3 and the wider Brighton & Hove postcode area. Specific neighbourhoods we move in and out of every month:

  • Central Brighton — The Lanes, North Laine, Brunswick, West Pier conservation area, Old Steine, Old Town.
  • Kemp Town — Kemp Town village, Sussex Square, Lewes Crescent. The Regency terraces here are beautiful and impossible to park outside without a permit suspension — we arrange that for you 5–10 working days ahead.
  • Hove — Brunswick, Adelaide, Palmeira Square, Hove Park, Stanford Avenue, Aldrington, Portslade-by-Sea.
  • Preston Park & Fiveways — family-home territory, easier parking, usually a 3-bed semi or detached.
  • Seven Dials, Montpelier & Clifton Hill — tall terraced houses on steep streets. We often shuttle to a smaller van where the main lorry can’t turn.
  • Hanover & Queen’s Park — narrow terraces, on-street permit parking only. Always survey before quoting.
  • Patcham, Withdean, Westdene — northern suburbs, easier access, typical family-home work.
  • Saltdean, Rottingdean, Ovingdean — coastal villages east of Brighton, often retirement and downsizer moves.
  • Falmer & Stanmer Park — student halls at the Universities of Sussex and Brighton.

What a Brighton removal day looks like with us

  1. Survey. We come to you. Walk every room, note access constraints, photograph fragile items, agree what’s moving. Free, no obligation. Quote emailed within 48 hours.
  2. Parking suspensions arranged. If your street is permit-only (most central Brighton streets are), we apply for a council suspension 5–10 working days ahead so the lorry isn’t ticketed.
  3. Pack day (optional). Our packing team comes 1–2 days before move day if you’ve booked the packing service.
  4. Move day. Crew arrives 8.00–8.30. Floor protection laid down. Furniture pad-wrapped in situ. Loaded in a stack-safe sequence.
  5. Drive to new property. We plan around Brighton’s notorious peak-time traffic (8am, 5pm) and the Lewes Road / Old Shoreham Road junctions.
  6. Unload and place. Items unwrapped only in the room where they’ll live. Boxes placed by colour-coded label.
  7. Walk-around with you. Tick off the inventory. Confirm any post-move arrangements.

Common Brighton move types

Studio & 1-bed flats

The Brighton bread-and-butter. Half-day with two crew. Most go to Hove, Portslade or out to the suburbs.

Terraced family homes

3- or 4-bed terraces in Hanover, Queen’s Park, Fiveways. Typically a full day, three crew, occasionally a shuttle van.

Brighton ↔ London relocations

One of our most common routes. Usually a one-day move with an overnight stay. We know the M23 / A23 corridor like the back of our hand.

Student moves

Sussex Uni, University of Brighton. Hall-to-hall, hall-to-home. See our student page.

Downsizer & senior moves

Often from a family Hove townhouse to a Saltdean or Rottingdean bungalow. Our full-pack + unpack service is the most-chosen option here.

Coastal ↔ country

Brighton out to Mid-Sussex (Lewes, Haywards Heath, Burgess Hill). Single-day moves. We do these multiple times a week.

Brighton-specific things we plan around

  • Resident parking permits. Most of central Brighton is permit-controlled. We arrange suspensions with the council in advance.
  • Steep streets. From Albion Hill to Elm Grove, the gradient affects lorry choice. We pick the right vehicle for the gradient.
  • Conservation areas. Brunswick, West Pier, Old Town — restrictions on signage and lorry-side branding in some streets. We comply with all council notices.
  • Beach-front congestion. Sunday-afternoon Madeira Drive is no place for a removal lorry. We plan move times to avoid the worst of it.
  • Race weekend, Pride and Brighton Marathon. Certain weekends are no-go for removals on the seafront. We’ll flag it at booking.

Why people choose us for Brighton removals

Three things to look at when choosing a Sussex remover:

  • BAR membership. The British Association of Removers audits members annually and runs an Advance Payment Guarantee for deposits. We’re full BAR members. Many cheaper operators are not.
  • Directly-employed crew. Not casual day labour. Our team train at our own training centre — one of only a handful of UK movers that has one.
  • Pad-wrap as standard. Every piece of furniture wrapped in heavy quilted blankets before it leaves your room — not in the lorry. This is the single biggest reason for our damage-free record over 40 years.

Frequently asked questions

Do you arrange parking suspensions in Brighton?

Yes — included as standard. We apply to Brighton & Hove City Council 5–10 working days ahead. You don’t need to do anything; we handle the form and the fee.

Can you move me on a Saturday?

Yes — Saturday moves are slightly more expensive (typically 10–15% surcharge) but very popular. Book 6+ weeks ahead for end-of-month Saturdays.

Do you cover the BN postcodes outside central Brighton?

Yes — full Brighton & Hove plus the coast east to Newhaven and west to Worthing. See the areas covered page for the full radius.

Do you do student moves at Sussex and Brighton Universities?

Yes — routinely. Falmer halls, Moulsecoomb, the various private student blocks. See our student removals page.

My flat is on the fourth floor with no lift. Is that a problem?

No, we do it often. Stairs add roughly 15–25% to the labour cost — we’ll quote it in the survey. We also have hoist contractors for impossible-stair items.
