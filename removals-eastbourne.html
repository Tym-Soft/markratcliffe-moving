Our Eastbourne removal service in detail

Free pre-move survey

Every removal starts with a free survey — either in your home or via video call, whichever you prefer. We walk through every room, agree what is moving and what is going to storage or charity, identify access constraints (parking, lifts, low beams), and discuss whether you want us to pack everything or just the fragile items. You then receive an itemised written quote with absolutely no obligation.

Premium full pad-wrap protection

This is what makes a Mark Ratcliffe move different from a generic removal. Every piece of furniture — sideboards, wardrobes, dining tables, bedside cabinets, the lot — is individually wrapped in a thick quilted blanket inside your home before it leaves the room. We tape it, we label it, we carry it to the lorry, and it is only unwrapped once it has been placed in its final position in your new property. Many of our customers tell us the absence of chips, scratches or dings is the single biggest difference they noticed compared with a previous remover.

Trained, uniformed crews

Our removers are trained in-house at our dedicated staff training centre to BS 8564-aligned standards. That covers fragile packing, furniture lifting and handling, vehicle loading, customer service and health-and-safety. They arrive on move day in branded uniforms, in immaculate company vans — small details, but they reflect how we run the rest of the operation.

Guaranteed move date

When you pay your 20% deposit, your move date is locked in. No slippage, no surprises. And critically — there is no charge if your completion date moves, if your buyer pulls out, or if you need to cancel. Removals customers across Eastbourne are often surprised when we explain this; many removal firms levy substantial change fees. We do not.

Pricing — how an Eastbourne removal quote is built

We do not believe in misleading "from £" headline prices. Every move is different, so every quote is built around your specific circumstances. The main factors are: the volume of your possessions (measured in cubic feet during the survey), the distance between properties, the access at both ends, whether you want a packing service, and whether you have any special items (pianos, antiques, safes, hot tubs).

For a sense of guideline pricing, see our full Eastbourne removals cost guide. As a quick reference: a 2-bedroom local Eastbourne move typically runs £550–£900; a 3-bedroom move £800–£1,400; a 4-bedroom-plus move £1,200–£2,200. Long-distance and international moves are quoted separately.

What happens on the day of your Eastbourne removal

For most customers, removal day is the part they have most anxiety about. The honest reality is that if the survey, packing and planning have all been done properly, move day itself is the easiest part. Here is how a typical Eastbourne move runs with us.

Arrival, 8.00–8.30am. Two or three of our liveried lorries pull up at your property. The crew lead introduces the team, walks the rooms with you, confirms the destination address and any final additions, and lays out floor protection on hard floors and stair carpets.

Loading, 9.00am onwards. The pre-packed cartons load first — they stack heaviest on the bottom and travel well. Pad-wrapped furniture follows, with the items going into the new property last loaded last (so they are first off at the other end). Mattresses and sofas travel in protective bags. The crew keeps a running inventory as items go on board.

Final walk-through, lunchtime. Before the lorry pulls away, we walk every room of your old home with you — including loft, garage and shed — to confirm nothing is left behind. We take photographs of the empty rooms for your records. You lock up and the crew heads to the new address.

Unloading, afternoon. At the new property, the crew lays floor protection again, takes the inventory in reverse order, places each piece of furniture in the room you have nominated, and only then removes the pad-wrap. Boxes go to the rooms you indicate. Beds are reassembled. By the end of unloading, everything is unwrapped, placed and accounted for.

Sign-off. Final walk-through, you sign off the inventory, balance payment is settled, and the crew heads home. A typical Eastbourne 3-bedroom move runs 8am to 4pm — though this varies with distance, volume and access.

Special items we handle in Eastbourne

Most removal companies handle standard furniture and boxes well; it is the awkward items that separate good firms from great ones. Over forty years we have moved every variety of awkward thing across Sussex, and our crews are equipped to handle:

Pianos

Upright and grand pianos require specific lifting technique, a piano dolly, climbing belts and at least four crew. We have moved pianos through bay windows, up spiral staircases and into the back of houses without external access. Always quote ahead — pianos are never an add-on the day of.

Antiques and art

Custom-built timber crates for valuable paintings, sculptures and mirrors. For genuinely high-value antiques we recommend a specialist art shipper — and we will tell you honestly when that is the right answer rather than us.

Safes and gun cabinets

Up to around 250kg we can move with our own equipment; above that we partner with a specialist heavy-lift firm. We will recommend the right route during the survey.

Hot tubs and garden buildings

Hot tubs need to be drained, disconnected and lifted — typically a specialist sub-contractor. Sheds, summer houses and garden offices often dismantle and travel in panels. We coordinate.

Wine collections

Temperature-controlled transport for serious collections. Standard transport for everyday wine. We can also store wine in our climate-stable Prestige rooms between properties.

Pets and plants

Pets and plants travel best with you in your own vehicle. We will happily help plan logistics — including kennels or specialist pet transport — and we can pack and ship potted plants where local rules allow.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much do removals cost in Eastbourne?

Eastbourne removal costs vary by volume, distance and packing service. As a guide, a 2-bedroom local Eastbourne move typically runs £550–£900 and a 3-bedroom move £800–£1,400. Our full cost guide breaks down every factor.

How far in advance should I book my Eastbourne move?

We recommend 4–6 weeks during the peak May–September season. For off-peak moves we can often book within 1–2 weeks. A 20% deposit locks in your guaranteed move date.

Are you BAR registered?

Yes — Mark Ratcliffe Moving is a member of the British Association of Removers Overseas Group, which means we are independently inspected against BAR standards for premises, vehicles, training and customer service.

Do you offer same-day or next-day removals in Eastbourne?

For smaller man-and-van jobs we sometimes can. Full house removals require survey and packing time so we usually need at least a few days' notice — give us a call and we will tell you what is honestly possible.

What happens if my move date changes?

There is no charge for postponements, cancellations or key waits. We simply update the diary at no extra cost — one of the things our Eastbourne customers tell us they value most.
